Information and guidance for pursuing AuD and PhD degrees

AuD/PhD Pathways

There are several routes to obtaining both an AuD (Doctor of Audiology) and PhD degree. Understanding these pathways can help you make informed decisions about your educational journey.

Simultaneous Programs

Some institutions offer joint AuD/PhD programs where students pursue both degrees concurrently. This pathway typically takes 5-7 years to complete and allows for integration of clinical training with research methodology.

  • Efficient use of time by completing both degrees together
  • Coordinated curriculum that bridges clinical and research training
  • Often includes funding support throughout the program
  • Requires careful time management and dedication
  • Students develop both skill sets simultaneously, allowing for immediate application

Sequential Programs

Many professionals complete one degree before pursuing the other. This can be done in either order: AuD first, then PhD, or PhD first, then AuD.

  • AuD then PhD: Gain clinical experience before focusing on research. Clinical insights can inform research questions and make you a more effective researcher.
  • PhD then AuD: Develop research expertise before clinical training. Strong research foundation can enhance evidence-based clinical practice.
  • Allows for work experience between degrees, providing perspective and clarity on career goals
  • May take longer overall but offers flexibility in career development and financial planning
  • Opportunity to explore each field deeply before committing to the other

Same vs. Different Institutions

Students may complete both degrees at the same institution or attend different schools for each degree. Each approach has distinct advantages:

  • Same Institution: Established relationships with faculty, familiarity with resources, potential for streamlined processes, consistent mentorship throughout your training
  • Different Institutions: Exposure to diverse perspectives, broader professional network, varied research experiences, opportunity to work with different equipment and methodologies

Funding Opportunities

Various funding mechanisms support students pursuing AuD and PhD degrees. Understanding these options early can help you plan your financial strategy:

  • NIH F31: Predoctoral fellowship for PhD students conducting health-related research. Provides stipend and tuition support.
  • NIH F32: Postdoctoral fellowship for recent PhD graduates continuing research training.
  • NSF GRFP: Graduate Research Fellowship Program supporting STEM graduate students with three years of funding.
  • HHF ERG: Hearing Health Foundation Emerging Research Grants for innovative hearing and balance research.
  • Institutional Funding: Teaching assistantships, research assistantships, and departmental scholarships vary by institution.
  • Professional Organizations: AAA, AAS, and other organizations offer student scholarships and research grants.
  • Diversity Fellowships: Many programs specifically support underrepresented students in STEM and healthcare fields.

Example Pathways

Example 1: Simultaneous Program

"I entered a joint AuD/PhD program directly after completing my undergraduate degree. Over 6 years, I completed clinical rotations while also conducting dissertation research. The integrated curriculum allowed me to apply research principles to clinical questions immediately. I defended my dissertation in year 6 and graduated with both degrees. The streamlined timeline was challenging but incredibly rewarding, and I entered the job market with both clinical skills and a strong research portfolio."

Example 2: Sequential (AuD First)

"After earning my AuD and working as a clinical audiologist for 3 years, I returned to pursue a PhD. My clinical experience helped me identify research questions that mattered to practitioners and patients. I saw firsthand the gaps between research and practice, which motivated my dissertation topic. I completed my PhD in 4 years while maintaining some clinical work, which kept me grounded in real-world applications. The clinical experience made me a better researcher because I understood what questions clinicians actually needed answered."

Example 3: Sequential (PhD First)

"I completed my PhD in hearing science first, developing expertise in auditory electrophysiology and computational modeling. After several years in research, I decided clinical training would enhance my research program and help me better understand how my findings applied to patient care. I pursued an AuD as a post-PhD professional, which gave me a strong research foundation before developing clinical skills. Now I run a research lab while also seeing patients, and each domain informs the other in valuable ways."

Example 4: Different Institutions

"I completed my AuD at a large research university with strong clinical training, then moved to a different institution for my PhD to work with a specific advisor whose research aligned perfectly with my interests. The transition exposed me to different departmental cultures, research approaches, and professional networks. While it required adjustment, having two institutional homes has been invaluable for my career. I have collaborators and mentors at both places, and I learned that different programs have unique strengths."

Planning Your Journey

As you consider your path to obtaining both degrees, reflect on these questions:

  • What are your long-term career goals? (Academic research, clinical practice, industry, hybrid roles)
  • Do you prefer focused immersion in one degree at a time, or do you thrive with variety?
  • What funding opportunities are available to you?
  • What institutions offer programs aligned with your research interests?
  • Are there geographic or personal factors that influence your decision?
  • Do you want to work between degrees, or continue your education without interruption?

There is no single "right" path—the best choice depends on your individual circumstances, goals, and preferences. Connect with members in our directory to learn about their experiences and get advice tailored to your situation.
